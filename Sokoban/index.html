You

Warehouse keeper, charged with moving crates to designated storage areas.
Floor

You can walk, and push crates over this surface.
Wall

Crate

A container filled with extremely valueable goods. Containers are heavy, but can be pushed around if you put your weight behind one. Crates belong in designated storage areas.
Designated Storage Area

This is where crates need to go, but you can take a crate out of a designated area temporarily too. Just remember to return it there before you go.

SOKOBAN

The kudos goes to the Thinking Rabit, Unbalance, and countless others that worked hard and inspired the likes of me to recreate this timelsess gem of puzzle-solving. I got the bug when I was about 10 years old and I wrote a crude imlementation of this game in BASIC, in ASCII. I could not let the young me upstage the current me, so here we are!

Anime/Pixelart

Dear artists! Thank You for allowing me to see the art you make, and inspiring me to follow your light. I hope you don't mind a little harmless worshipping on my part. None of the characters in this game were created from scratch; instead, they were creatively inspired by the works of others.

Font

If you're looking for any kind of "old-school" font, hit this link: int10h.org. I dabbled in font-making myself, but this is just pure wizardry! I used their Web437_IBM_VGA_8x16.woff font for this game. Also, can we agreee that their website just looks fantastic?

BeepBox

I can't imagine the art of chiptunes without beepbox. I am using their JS library to make you feel it is 1980s all over again!

Music

I tried writing my own music, but then I found this tune on BeepBox by SofaKing and I stopped trying. Not because I suck, but because he rules. But also, I suck.

MD5

This hashing marvel by Joseph Myers is used to store your progress. Each time you beat a level, the level's MD5 hash is stored in your cookies along with the number of steps you took. This way, it is not the name that matters, but the path you took!

Compression

Level hashes are compressed by a very simple algorithm that I wrote. It is fast, reliable and compresses strings of repeating letters like "WWWWWW" to "W6", which is all I needed. Compression rate is around 69%! (And the compressed size is never larger than the original, wich is sometimes the case with other Big Name copression algorithms!)

Pathfinding

Every time you click on a traversable area a kitten dies!
Just kidding! An algorithm that I wrote (that is very loosely inspired by Edsger W. Dijkstra's shoretest path algorithm) guides you to your destination using the shortest available path! No kittens were harmed, but some were fed canned food during the implementation of this algorithm.
